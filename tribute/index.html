About Piłecki

Witold Pilecki (13 May 1901 – 25 May 1948) was a resistance leader, co-founding the Secret Polish Army in November 1939.

A timeline of Witold's life:

  • 1901 - Born in Olonets, Karelia
  • 1910 - Moved to Wilno
  • 1919 - Enlisted in the Polish volunteer army
  • 1925 - Assigned to the 26th Lancer Regiment with the rank of Ensign
  • 1931 - Married Maria Ostrowska
  • 1938 - Received the Silver Cross of Merit for his activism
  • 1939 - Arrested and sent to Auschwitz
  • 1943 - Escaped Auschwitz with two other comrades, taking stolen German documents
  • 1944 - Took part in the Warsaw Uprising, arrested by the Germans the same year
  • 1945 - Released from liberation of the POW camp
  • 1947 - Arrested and tortured by communist authorities
  • 1948 - Executed in Mokotow Prison
